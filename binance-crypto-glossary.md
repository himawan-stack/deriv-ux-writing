# Binance crypto glossary

A reference of crypto and blockchain terminology, based on the Binance Academy glossary at https://www.binance.com/en/academy/glossary. All definitions have been paraphrased and are intended as a working reference for writers and researchers. For the canonical, expanded explanations and any updates, see the source.

> **Note for Deriv context:** This is an industry reference, not a Deriv terminology source. For Deriv-approved product terms, see `product-glossary.md`.

---

## #

### 51% Attack
Also called a majority attack. Happens when a single miner — or a group acting together — controls more than half of a network's mining hash rate or computational power, giving them the ability to manipulate the chain.

---

## A

### Absolute Advantage
An economics idea describing a situation where one party can produce a good or service more efficiently than another.

### Active Management
An investment approach where fund managers actively pick assets and time trades in an attempt to beat a benchmark index or the broader market.

### Actively Validated Services (AVS)
Systems kept under continuous monitoring and validation to confirm they're working correctly.

### Ad Hoc
A Latin phrase used in English to mean "for this specific purpose."

### Address
A string of text identifying a wallet's location on the blockchain. It's typically a hashed form of the wallet's public key.

### Aevo (AEVO)
A decentralised exchange that focuses on derivatives trading.

### Airdrop
A distribution of tokens to the public — often given to holders of another token or to wallet addresses that are active on a particular blockchain.

### Algorithm
A defined sequence of steps used to solve a problem or perform a calculation.

### All or None Order (AON)
An order that must be filled completely or not at all — partial fills aren't permitted.

### All-Time High (ATH)
The highest price an asset has ever reached over its trading history.

### Allocation
A portion of tokens or equity earmarked for a specific investor, team, group, or organisation. May be earned, purchased, or simply reserved.

### Alpha
The earliest version of a product released by a team — a basic, primitive build, before any beta release.

### Altcoin
Any cryptocurrency other than Bitcoin.

### Angel Investor
A wealthy individual who provides funding to entrepreneurs or early-stage start-ups.

### Anti Money Laundering (AML)
The legal and regulatory framework designed to detect and limit the movement of funds from illegal or suspicious sources.

### Application Programming Interface (API)
A set of functions and procedures that let users interact with an application or service programmatically — for example, to execute exchange features through code.

### Application-Specific Integrated Circuit (ASIC)
A chip purpose-built for one specific task, optimised for performance in that task rather than for general use.

### Arbitrage
Buying an asset in one market and selling it in another to profit from price differences.

### ARC-20
A token standard for fungible tokens (sometimes called coloured coins) on the Bitcoin network.

### Artificial Intelligence (AI)
Intelligence demonstrated by machines or computer systems that simulate human cognitive processes.

### ASIC-resistant
A cryptocurrency designed so that ASIC mining hardware either can't be used effectively or offers no meaningful advantage over GPU mining.

### Ask Price
The lowest price a seller is prepared to accept when listing an asset on an exchange.

### Asset Management
The practice of managing assets — either on a client's behalf or one's own.

### Assets Under Management (AUM)
The total market value of financial assets that an entity manages on behalf of clients. A common indicator of an institution's scale and influence.

### Asynchronous
In electronics, events that don't happen simultaneously or in sync with a main program flow.

### Atomic Swap
Smart contract technology that lets two parties exchange different cryptocurrencies directly, without going through a centralised intermediary.

### Attack surface
The collection of points in a software system where an attacker might attempt to enter or extract data.

### Auction
A live event in which assets or services are sold to the highest bidder, typically led by an auctioneer.

### Automated Market Maker (AMM)
A type of decentralised exchange protocol that lets users trade digital assets without an intermediary, using algorithmic pricing instead of order books.

## B

### B-Tokens
A category of fully-collateralised wrapped tokens issued by Binance.

### Bags
The collection of coins or tokens a person holds. Often used to refer to a portfolio of poorly performing assets that the holder continues to keep.

### Beacon Chain
Ethereum's proof-of-stake consensus layer, which coordinates the validators who secure the network in exchange for staking rewards.

### Bear Flag Pattern
A short consolidation within a strong downtrend, often signalling a continuation to lower prices.

### Bear Market
A sustained period of falling prices. Used in crypto and traditional markets alike.

### Benchmark
A standard used to measure the performance of an asset or portfolio against.

### BEP-1155
A token standard on BNB Chain optimised for creating and managing digital assets efficiently.

### BEP-20
The token standard used on BNB Smart Chain.

### BEP-721
A technical standard defining how non-fungible tokens are issued on BNB Smart Chain.

### BEP-95
A BNB Evolution Proposal that adds a real-time token-burning mechanism to BNB Chain.

### Beta (Coefficient)
A measure of how volatile an asset is relative to a market index or reference portfolio.

### Beta (Release)
An early version of software released for testing and feedback. It follows the alpha stage.

### Bid Price
In financial markets, the price a buyer is willing to pay for an asset.

### Bid-Ask Spread
The gap between the highest bid and the lowest ask price on the order book for a given asset.

### Binance Ecosystem Fund (BEF)
A Binance initiative for collaborating with partners committed to the blockchain and crypto ecosystem.

### Binancian
Someone actively engaged with or contributing to the Binance exchange.

### Bitcoin
The first cryptocurrency, created by the pseudonymous Satoshi Nakamoto. Originally described as peer-to-peer electronic cash.

### Bitcoin Core
The main software implementation for interacting with the Bitcoin network. First released by Satoshi.

### Bitcoin Dominance
The ratio of Bitcoin's market capitalisation to the total market cap of all cryptocurrencies combined.

### Bitcoin Maximalists
People who believe Bitcoin is the only cryptocurrency worth holding and will form the basis of future global finance.

### Bitcoin Pizza
The first known purchase of physical goods with Bitcoin. Laszlo Hanyecz famously bought two pizzas for 10,000 BTC.

### Black Swan Event
An unexpected event with significant and far-reaching consequences, often diverging sharply from forecasts.

### Block
A file containing transaction data. Blocks are linked in sequence to form a blockchain.

### Block Explorer
A web tool for browsing blockchain data — blocks, transactions, balances, and history.

### Block header
A section of a block holding its metadata and a summary of its transactions. It's the part that gets hashed during mining.

### Block Height
The number of blocks between any given block and the very first block (the genesis block, often called block 0).

### Block Reward
The amount of coins awarded by a blockchain protocol to a miner for successfully mining a new block.

### Blockchain
A decentralised, digital ledger that records transactions in chronological order.

### Blockchain Charity Foundation
A decentralised charity platform promoting the idea of blockchain for social good — the first of its kind.

### Bloom Filter
A data structure that can quickly indicate whether a particular item is likely part of a set.

### Blue-Chip Token
A cryptocurrency that has proven its staying power over time — well-established and consistently valuable.

### BNB
The native token of BNB Chain.

### BNSOL
A liquid staking token representing staked SOL on Binance.

### Bollinger Bands
A technical analysis tool that measures volatility using two bands around a simple moving average.

### Bond
A fixed-income investment representing a loan made to a borrower — usually a government or company.

### Bounty
A reward offered by an individual or group to incentivise specific work, behaviour, or development.

### BRC-20 Tokens
An experimental token standard that uses the Ordinals protocol to mint and transfer fungible tokens on Bitcoin.

### Break-Even Point (BEP)
The point at which total costs equal total revenue or current value — no profit, no loss.

### Breakeven Multiple
The multiple by which an asset's current price would need to rise to return to its previous all-time high.

### Breakout
When an asset's price moves outside a defined range or pattern — typically beyond support or resistance.

### BscScan
A block explorer designed for BNB Smart Chain.

### BTC Wallet Address
A unique alphanumeric string used to send and receive Bitcoin transactions.

### BUIDL
A play on "HODL" — a call to focus on building products rather than just holding tokens.

### Bull Flag Pattern
A short consolidation within a strong uptrend, often signalling further upside.

### Bull Market
A sustained period of rising prices. Used in crypto and traditional markets.

### Burner Wallet
A temporary crypto wallet used for risky or one-off interactions.

### Buy Wall
A very large buy order, or a cluster of buy orders at the same price, sitting in the order book.

## C

### Candidate Block
A temporary block a miner builds in an attempt to add it to the blockchain and claim the reward.

### Candlestick
A chart format showing the open, close, high, and low for an asset within a given period.

### Capitulation
A phase of heavy selling, when investors give up and exit positions as quickly as possible.

### Censorship-resistance
The property of a network that prevents any entity from altering or blocking its transactions.

### Central Bank
A financial institution that acts as a country's monetary authority — managing currency, interest rates, and money supply.

### Central Bank Digital Currency (CBDC)
A digital form of money issued and sanctioned by a country's central bank. Can be designed for wholesale or retail use.

### Central Processing Unit (CPU)
The component of a computer that interprets and executes program instructions.

### Centralized
Describes a system where decision-making and control are concentrated at a single point.

### Centralized Exchange
A traditional crypto marketplace known for high liquidity and advanced trading tools — though it comes with custodial risks.

### ChatGPT
A natural-language processing model developed by OpenAI — a major advance in conversational AI.

### Cipher
A method for encrypting and decrypting messages. Ciphers can be symmetric or asymmetric depending on their key model.

### Circulating Supply
An estimate of how many coins or tokens of a given cryptocurrency are publicly available and actively traded.

### Cloud
A shared pool of computing resources made available to multiple users via the internet.

### Coin
A standalone cryptocurrency that operates on its own native blockchain, used as a medium of exchange.

### Cold Storage
Keeping digital assets offline to reduce the risk of theft or compromise.

### Collateral
Something of value pledged against a loan as a guarantee of repayment.

### Colocation
Dedicated server space within a stock exchange's data centre, often used by high-frequency traders.

### Commodity Futures Trading Commission (CFTC)
The US agency that regulates derivatives markets, including futures, options, and swaps.

### Compound Interest
Interest calculated on both the original principal and accumulated interest from earlier periods — accelerating returns over time.

### Confirmation Time
How long it takes for a transaction to be approved and added to the blockchain ledger.

### Confluence
When several indicators, signals, or methods point to the same conclusion — producing a more reliable trading strategy.

### Consensus Algorithm
A mechanism that lets computers in a blockchain network agree on the truth of the data, even when some participants may act dishonestly.

### Consumer Price Index (CPI)
An indicator that tracks inflation by measuring price changes over time across a basket of goods and services.

### Contango and Backwardation
Terms describing the relationship between a commodity's futures price and its expected future spot price — useful for trading, hedging, and arbitrage.

### Copy Trading
A strategy where one trader's positions are automatically mirrored by other users.

### Counterparty Risk
The risk that one party in a financial transaction fails to honour their obligations, causing losses for the other.

### Credentials
Personal identifying information such as usernames, passwords, and email addresses.

### Cross-Chain Bridges
Tools that connect different blockchain networks, allowing assets to move between them and supporting interoperability.

### Crypto ETFs
Exchange-traded funds that give investors exposure to crypto price movements without requiring them to hold the assets directly.

### Crypto Fear and Greed Index
A sentiment indicator that scores overall crypto market mood from 0 to 100 using multiple inputs.

### Crypto Protocol
The rules and procedures that govern how participants behave in a decentralised network.

### Crypto Winter
A prolonged period of falling or stagnant crypto prices accompanied by negative sentiment.

### Cryptocurrency
A digital currency secured by cryptography, designed to function as a peer-to-peer medium of exchange.

### Cryptography
The discipline of using mathematics and computation to encrypt and decrypt information.

### Custody
The act of holding assets on behalf of another party — or the holding of one's own assets.

## D

### Daemon
A background process that waits for a specific trigger before activating.

### Danksharding
An approach to sharding that improves how Ethereum manages data and processes transactions.

### Dead Cat Bounce
A brief upward move in the price of a falling asset, soon followed by a continued decline.

### Death Cross
A chart pattern signalling potential further declines, formed when a short-term moving average crosses below a longer-term one.

### Decentralized Application (DApp)
An application running on a peer-to-peer network rather than relying on a single server. Users typically connect using crypto wallets.

### Decentralized Autonomous Cooperative (DAC)
An organisation governed by its shareholders rather than a central authority.

### Decentralized Autonomous Organization (DAO)
A system of hard-coded rules that determine how a decentralised organisation operates and makes decisions.

### Decentralized Exchange (DEX)
An exchange where users trade directly from their own wallets — no deposits, no custody by the exchange.

### Decentralized Finance (DeFi)
The ecosystem of financial applications built on blockchain networks, operating without centralised intermediaries.

### Decentralized Indexes
Investment vehicles that track the performance of multiple cryptocurrencies or digital assets traded on decentralised exchanges.

### Decryption
The reverse of encryption — converting unreadable ciphertext back into readable plaintext.

### Deep Web
The portion of the internet that isn't indexed by standard search engines like Google.

### Delisting
Removing an asset from an exchange, either at the project team's request or the exchange's decision.

### Depeg
When a stablecoin loses its peg to its reference asset. Understanding causes and prevention is critical for maintaining trust.

### Depression
A severe, prolonged downturn in economic activity.

### Design Flaw Attack
An attack in which a malicious user deliberately creates a smart contract or market with known flaws to exploit other users.

### Diamond Hands
Holding an asset regardless of how volatile its price gets.

### Difficulty
The amount of work needed to mine a block on a proof-of-work blockchain.

### Difficulty Bomb
A mechanism that gradually increases mining difficulty on Ethereum, supporting its transition to proof of stake.

### Digital Asset
Anything created, stored, and accessed in digital form.

### Digital Signature
A cryptographic mechanism that verifies the authenticity and integrity of digital data, including crypto transactions.

### Direct Market Access (DMA)
Technology that lets investors trade directly against order books without going through a broker.

### Divergence
When an asset's price and a technical indicator (such as RSI or MACD) move in opposite directions.

### Diversification
Spreading funds across different asset types and jurisdictions to reduce overall risk.

### Do Your Own Research (DYOR)
A common piece of advice — research a project yourself before trusting others' opinions.

### Dollar Cost Averaging (DCA)
Investing the same amount at regular intervals regardless of the asset's current price.

### Dot Plot
A simple chart where each data point appears as a single dot.

### Double Spending
Spending the same coins more than once — typically the result of a race attack or 51% attack.

## E

### Eclipse Attack
An attack where a majority of malicious peers surround a network node, cutting it off from honest information.

### Efficient Market Hypothesis (EMH)
An economic theory holding that asset prices reflect all available information at any given moment.

### EigenLayer
An Ethereum protocol that introduces the concept of "restaking collective."

### EIP
Ethereum Improvement Proposals — formal documents describing proposed upgrades or new features.

### EIP-1559
A 2021 upgrade that overhauled Ethereum's fee mechanism.

### EIP-3074
A proposal to improve how transactions are authorised on Ethereum.

### EIP-4844
Also known as proto-danksharding. Aims to reduce Ethereum fees and increase transaction throughput.

### EIP-7251
A proposal to modify Ethereum staking by raising the maximum effective validator balance.

### EIP-7702
A proposal to enhance the functionality of externally owned accounts on Ethereum.

### Elasticity
A measure of how responsive one variable is to changes in another.

### Encryption
Converting information into a secure form to prevent unauthorised access.

### Endogenous Variable
A variable in an economic model whose value is shaped by other variables in the same model.

### Enterprise Ethereum Alliance (EEA)
The first global standards body focused on enterprise Ethereum — aiming to accelerate adoption through open, standards-based architecture.

### ERC-1155
A versatile token standard suited for gaming, digital collectibles, and managing multiple token types efficiently.

### ERC-20
The technical standard for fungible tokens on Ethereum, proposed by Fabian Vogelsteller in November 2015.

### ERC-404
An experimental token standard that blends fungibility and non-fungibility.

### ERC-721
The Ethereum standard for non-fungible tokens — each token is unique and not interchangeable.

### ETF
Exchange-traded funds. In the crypto market, they offer exposure to assets without direct ownership, with their own set of benefits, risks, and impacts.

### Ethereum Classic
The original Ethereum chain that persisted after the 2016 DAO-hack hard fork. Unlike Ethereum, it still uses proof of work.

### Ethereum Foundation
A non-profit dedicated to developing, improving, and promoting the Ethereum blockchain.

### Ethereum Virtual Machine (EVM)
A Turing-complete machine that runs Ethereum smart contracts, which are typically written in Solidity.

### Exchange
A marketplace where users can buy and sell cryptocurrencies.

### Exogenous Variable
A variable in an economic model that influences the model but is determined by outside factors.

### Exponential Moving Average (EMA)
A moving average that gives more weight to recent price data, used in trading analysis.

### Externally Owned Account (EOA)
A type of Ethereum account controlled by a private key, as opposed to a smart contract.

## F

### Fakeout
A trade entered on the expectation of a price move that quickly reverses or doesn't materialise.

### Falling Knife
Buying an asset that's dropping fast, in the hope it will bounce back soon.

### Falling Wedge Pattern
A chart pattern of converging trend lines that often signals a bullish reversal.

### Fan Tokens
Digital assets created by sports teams, clubs, or brands to deepen fan engagement and generate revenue.

### Fear Of Missing Out (FOMO)
The anxiety that comes with feeling left behind on a potentially profitable opportunity.

### Fear, Uncertainty and Doubt (FUD)
The deliberate spreading of negativity to unsettle customers, traders, or investors.

### Fiat
Government-issued money declared as legal tender.

### Fibonacci Retracement
A technical analysis tool that uses Fibonacci ratios to identify potential support and resistance levels.

### Fill Or Kill Order (FOK)
An order that must be filled immediately and entirely, or else cancelled outright.

### Finality
The guarantee that a completed transaction can't be altered, reversed, or cancelled.

### First-Mover Advantage (FMA)
The competitive edge gained by being first to bring a product or service to a new market.

### Fiscal Policy
How a government adjusts tax rates to manage how public funds are collected and spent.

### Flappening
The moment Litecoin surpassed Bitcoin Cash in market capitalisation.

### Flashbots
A research and development organisation focused on improving MEV-related blockchain solutions on Ethereum.

### Flippening
The hypothetical (or future) moment when Ethereum surpasses Bitcoin in market capitalisation.

### Flow Variable
A measurement of something over a specified period.

### Flux (FLUX)
A decentralised cloud infrastructure network designed for scalable Web3 applications.

### Forced Liquidation
The forced closure of a trader's leveraged position when it can no longer meet margin requirements.

### Forex (FX)
Short for foreign exchange — the global market where fiat currencies are traded.

### Fork
A split in a blockchain's transaction history. Can be either a soft fork or a hard fork.

### Formal Verification
Using rigorous mathematical proofs to confirm specific properties of cryptographic algorithms or blockchain mechanisms.

### Fraud Proof
Cryptographic evidence submitted by a verifier to challenge the validity of a transaction. Common in scaling solutions.

### Fren
Crypto slang for "friend" — a casual, friendly form of address that emerged in online communities.

### Full Node
A computer that fully enforces all rules of a blockchain and completely validates its transactions and blocks.

### Fully Diluted Valuation (FDV)
An estimate of what a project's market value would be if all its tokens were in circulation.

### Fundamental Analysis (FA)
Evaluating an asset's intrinsic value based on its underlying characteristics and properties.

### Funding Fees
Periodic payments exchanged between long and short traders in perpetual futures markets.

### Fungibility
The property of an asset whose individual units are interchangeable and identical in value and function.

### Futures Contract
A standardised contract to buy or sell an asset at a set price on a future date.

## G

### GameFi
A subset of DeFi that combines blockchain with gaming, often featuring play-to-earn mechanics powered by NFTs and cryptocurrencies.

### Gas
A pricing mechanism for the cost of executing smart contract operations and transactions on a network.

### Gas Limit
The maximum fee a user is willing to pay for a transaction or smart contract operation.

### GDP Deflator
A measure showing how prices in an economy have changed over time.

### General Public License
A licence allowing users to copy and modify software, on the condition that derivative works are distributed under the same licence.

### Genesis Block
The first block in any blockchain — also referred to as block 0 or block 1.

### GitHub
A platform for sharing, collaborating on, and storing source code — open source or proprietary.

### GM (Good Morning)
A greeting that's become a cultural marker of inclusivity and friendliness in the crypto community.

### Golden Cross
A bullish chart pattern formed when a shorter-term moving average crosses above a longer-term one.

### Golden Ratio
A mathematical proportion that appears throughout nature, art, architecture, and even financial markets.

### Goldilocks
An ideal state in economics — conditions that are neither overheating nor too cold.

### Gossip Protocol
An asynchronous peer-to-peer communication method between nodes in a distributed system.

### Gross Domestic Product (GDP)
A measure of the total value of goods and services produced in a country over a specified period.

### Gwei
A small denomination of ether, typically used when quoting gas prices.

## H

### Hackathon
A competition where developers build applications on top of a blockchain ecosystem.

### Hacker
A person with deep knowledge of computer systems and networks — including both programmers and cybersecurity professionals.

### Haha Money Printer Go Brrrrr
A meme that emerged in response to the US Federal Reserve's 2020 money-printing plans.

### Halving
The reduction of a proof-of-work cryptocurrency's block reward by 50%. Bitcoin's halving occurs roughly every four years.

### Hard Cap
The maximum amount a project plans to raise during an ICO or similar fundraising event.

### Hard Landing
When an economy moves rapidly from growth into a slowdown or recession.

### Hash
The output of a hash function applied to data. Also referred to as a hash value, hash code, or digest.

### Hash Rate
How quickly a computer can calculate new hashes.

### Hashed TimeLock Contract (HTLC)
A smart contract feature used to create modifiable payment channels.

### Herd Instinct
The tendency of some traders to follow the crowd rather than make independent decisions.

### High-Frequency Trading (HFT)
A type of algorithmic trading involving large volumes of orders executed in fractions of a second.

### HODL
Originally a typo of "hold" on bitcointalk, now also interpreted as "Hold On for Dear Life" — meaning to keep holding rather than sell.

### Honeypot
A security mechanism designed to detect or trap unauthorised access to a system.

## I

### Iceberg Order
A conditional order that breaks a large position into smaller chunks to hide the full size from the market.

### Immutability
The property of being unchangeable — a foundational principle of Bitcoin and blockchain technology.

### Index
A financial instrument used to track the price of an asset or a basket of assets.

### Index Funds
Investment funds designed to replicate the performance of a specific market index, such as the S&P 500.

### Initial Coin Offering (ICO)
A fundraising method in which a new project sells its cryptocurrency directly to investors.

### Initial DEX Offering (IDO)
A blockchain fundraising method where tokens are sold publicly on a decentralised exchange.

### Initial Exchange Offering (IEO)
A fundraising method that adds a trusted intermediary between the project team and token buyers, reducing investor risk.

### Initial Public Offering (IPO)
When a private company first offers its shares for public purchase.

### Inscription
Data attached to individual units of a cryptocurrency.

### Integrated Circuit (IC)
A small chip — usually silicon — containing transistors, resistors, capacitors, and other electronic components.

### Interest Rates
The cost of borrowing money or the return earned on an investment, expressed as a percentage of the principal.

### Interoperability
The ability of different blockchains to interact with one another and build on each other's features.

### InterPlanetary File System (IPFS)
An open-source protocol for distributed file storage and access.

### IOU
Stands for "I owe you" — an informal document acknowledging a debt.

### Isolated Margin
The portion of a margin balance assigned to a specific position, letting traders limit risk per trade.

### Issuance
The process of generating new cryptocurrency, which varies depending on the project's parameters.

## K

### Keccak
A cryptographic hash function created by Guido Bertoni, Joan Daemen, Michaël Peeters, and Gilles Van Assche.

### Know Your Customer (KYC)
A standard verification procedure used by financial companies to identify customers and meet AML compliance requirements.

## L

### Laspeyres Index
A tool for tracking changes in the cost of a basket of goods over time.

### Latency
The delay between sending a transaction to a network and its first confirmation.

### Law of Demand
The principle that the quantity of a good consumers will buy depends on its price.

### Layer 2
A secondary protocol built on top of an existing blockchain to improve scalability.

### Ledger
A book or digital file that records financial transactions.

### Leveraged Tokens
Crypto derivatives that give traders leveraged exposure to a cryptocurrency without needing to manage margin themselves.

### LFG
Crypto slang for "Let's F***ing Go" — an expression of enthusiasm.

### Library
A collection of stable resources — code, documentation, message templates, or executables — that can be reused.

### Lightning Network
A second-layer protocol built on top of Bitcoin that increases transaction speed between participating nodes.

### Limit Order
An order to buy or sell at a specified price or better.

### Linux
An open-source operating system created by Linus Torvalds in 1991, used widely across devices and servers.

### Liquid Staking
Tokenising staked assets so they remain liquid and usable elsewhere while still earning staking rewards.

### Liquid Staking Token (LST)
A token representing assets that have been staked on a blockchain network.

### Liquidity
The ease with which an asset can be bought or sold without significantly affecting its price.

### Liquidity Crisis
A financial situation where a person, organisation, or market faces a shortage of cash, making it hard to meet short-term obligations.

### Liquidity Provider
A person or entity that supplies buy and sell orders to financial markets, supporting market liquidity and efficiency.

### Liquidity Ratios
Financial metrics used to measure a company's ability to meet short-term obligations.

### Listing
The addition of an asset to an exchange — either at the project's request or by the exchange's decision.

## M

### Mainnet
A fully developed and live blockchain protocol where real transactions are broadcast, verified, and recorded.

### Mainnet Swap
When a coin moves from a third-party platform (like Ethereum) to its own native blockchain.

### Maker
A trader who places an order that doesn't fill immediately and stays in the order book waiting for a match.

### Malware
Software written to infiltrate and damage computer systems or networks.

### Margin Trading
Trading with borrowed funds. High-risk and recommended only for experienced traders.

### Market Capitalization
The total value of a coin's circulating supply, calculated as price multiplied by supply.

### Market Momentum
A market's tendency to keep moving in the same direction within a given time frame.

### Market Order
An order that executes immediately at the best price currently available in the order book.

### Masternode
A network node — typically requiring a minimum staked balance — that earns rewards for performing specialised functions.

### Matching Engine
The software that pairs buy and sell orders in financial markets.

### Maximum Supply
The maximum number of coins or tokens that will ever exist for a given cryptocurrency.

### Mempool
A node's holding area for unconfirmed transactions it has seen but that haven't yet been included in a block.

### Merged Mining
Mining two or more cryptocurrencies at the same time without reducing overall mining performance.

### Merkle Tree
A hash-based data structure that organises large amounts of data into a form that's efficient to verify.

### Metadata
Data that describes other data — for example, information about a specific transaction's features.

### Metaverse
A persistent, online, three-dimensional virtual environment that many see as central to the future of digital interaction.

### Microtransactions
Small financial transfers made using cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin or Ether.

### Mining
The process of verifying transactions on a blockchain and adding them to the ledger as new blocks.

### Mining Farm
A large concentration of mining hardware, usually housed in a dedicated warehouse or data centre.

### Minting
The creation of new tokens, adding them to a network's circulating supply. Conceptually similar to mining, but with key technical differences.

### Monetary Policy
The set of policies authorities use to manage a country's money supply and interest rates.

### Money Markets
Markets where short-term financial instruments — like T-bills and certificates of deposit — are traded.

### Monitoring Tag
A Binance label applied to assets considered more volatile or risky than the average listed token.

### Moon
Slang for an asset experiencing a powerful upward price trend.

### Moving Average Envelopes
A technical analysis tool using two bands placed around a central moving average.

### Moving Average Ribbon
A technical analysis tool made up of multiple moving averages of varying lengths.

### Mt. Gox
A now-defunct Japanese Bitcoin exchange that was once the largest in the world.

### Multisignature
A wallet setup requiring approval from multiple parties before a transaction can be broadcast.

### Mutant Ape Yacht Club (MAYC)
An NFT collection from Yuga Labs, expanding the Bored Ape ecosystem.

## N

### Nakamoto Consensus
The protocol used in some blockchain networks to reach consensus on the chain's state without relying on a central authority.

### Net Asset Value (NAV)
An entity's total assets minus its liabilities.

### NFT Floor Prices
The lowest price at which a buyer can acquire an NFT from a given collection.

### NFT Mystery Boxes
Digital collectibles that contain a randomised set of NFTs revealed only when the buyer opens them.

### NGMI
Crypto acronym for "Not Gonna Make It" — used to express pessimism about a project or outcome.

### Node
A participant on a blockchain network that communicates with others to maintain network integrity and security.

### Non-Fungible Token (NFT)
A cryptographic token representing a unique digital or real-world asset — not interchangeable with other tokens.

### Nonce
A single-use number or string generated for verification, used to prevent replay of past transactions.

## O

### OCO Order
A "One Cancels the Other" order combining a limit and a stop-limit order — only one of the two can execute.

### Off-Chain
Transactions or activities that happen outside the main blockchain, often used to improve scalability.

### Offline Signing Orchestrator (OSO)
An IBM-developed technology that strengthens security for crypto and digital assets stored in cold storage.

### Offshore account
An account held in a jurisdiction other than the holder's country of citizenship.

### On-Chain
Transactions and activities recorded directly on the blockchain, ensuring data transparency, security, and immutability.

### opBNB
A layer-2 scaling solution that offloads transaction processing from BNB Smart Chain.

### Open-Source Software (OSS)
Software released under a licence that gives anyone the right to use, modify, and distribute it freely.

### Opportunity Cost
The value of what you give up when you choose one option over another.

### Oracle
A third-party data source feeding outside information into smart contracts.

### ORC-20 Tokens
Bitcoin-based tokens implemented as JSON files inscribed onto satoshis with an Ordinal serial number — similar in concept to BRC-20.

### Order Book
A real-time list of outstanding buy and sell orders for a particular asset on an exchange.

### Ordinals
NFT-like items that can be inscribed and minted directly on the Bitcoin blockchain.

### Orphan Block
A block whose parent block is unknown. Formed in older versions of Bitcoin Core where ancestry data wasn't enforced.

## P

### PancakeSwap
A decentralised exchange using the AMM model, deployed across multiple blockchain networks and offering a range of DeFi products.

### Paper Wallet
A printed sheet containing a cryptocurrency address and its corresponding private key.

### Parallelization
A technique for processing multiple transactions at the same time.

### Passive Management
An investment strategy that tracks an existing index rather than relying on active market timing.

### Peer-to-Peer (P2P)
A setup where computers share workload or resources directly with one another, without a central server.

### Pegged Currency
A currency whose value is tied to another currency, commodity, or basket of currencies.

### Permissionless Blockchain
An open blockchain where anyone can join the consensus process without needing approval.

### Phishing
An attack where a bad actor tries to steal user credentials to gain unauthorised access to accounts.

### Plasma
An Ethereum off-chain scaling proposal aimed at significantly increasing transactions per second.

### Polkadot Crowdloan
The process of staking DOT to support projects competing in Polkadot Slot Auctions, with rewards from those projects in return.

### Ponzi Scheme
A scam that uses incoming investor funds to pay returns to earlier investors. Always to be avoided.

### Premining
Generating tokens before a cryptocurrency's official launch.

### Price Action
The movement of an asset's price over time — used by traders to identify trade setups on charts.

### Prisoner's Dilemma
A classic example of why two people might not cooperate even when it would benefit them both.

### Private Key
A long, secret number that lets a user sign transactions and generate receiving addresses.

### Private Sale
An early investment round restricted to strategic investors with significant capital.

### Progressive Web application (PWA)
A web-based application built with modern web technologies that follows core web standards.

### Proof of Attendance Protocol (POAP)
A protocol that issues digital badges to commemorate event attendance.

### Proof of Reserves (PoR)
Verification that an exchange holds enough assets to cover its users' balances.

### Proof of Stake (PoS)
A consensus mechanism that rewards validators based on the amount of cryptocurrency they have staked.

### Proof of Staked Authority (PoSA)
A hybrid mechanism combining proof of stake and proof of authority to improve security through balanced validation and equal staking.

### Proof of Work (PoW)
A consensus mechanism in which miners use specialised hardware to solve cryptographic puzzles to validate transactions and produce new blocks.

### Proposer-Builder Separation (PBS)
An Ethereum design separating block proposers from block builders to improve scalability.

### Proto-Danksharding
An interim Ethereum scaling solution that precedes full danksharding — itself the final phase of Ethereum 2.0.

### Pseudorandom
A property of a function that produces output appearing random enough to pass statistical randomness tests.

### Public Key
One half of a cryptographic key pair, used to encrypt messages or verify digital signatures. In crypto, it generally serves as a wallet address.

### Pump-and-dump
A market manipulation scheme that artificially inflates the price of an asset before dumping it, causing a sharp price collapse.

## Q

### Quantitative Easing (QE)
A central bank strategy aimed at stimulating an underperforming economy.

### Quantitative Tightening (QT)
A monetary policy used by central banks to reduce the money supply.

### Quantum Computing
Computing that uses particles in superposition — qubits — that can represent 0, 1, or both simultaneously.

---

## R

### Race attack
Two transactions created with the same funds at the same moment, in an attempt to double-spend.

### Ransomware
Malware that takes control of a computer and threatens to delete or expose data unless a ransom is paid.

### Real World Assets (RWAs)
Physical assets with intrinsic value — such as real estate, commodities, or art — represented on a blockchain through tokenisation.

### Recession
A sustained period of significant decline in economic activity.

### Recursive Inscription
A process that uses data from existing inscriptions to create new ones.

### Rekt
Crypto slang for being financially destroyed or liquidated.

### Relative Strength Index (RSI)
A technical indicator measuring market momentum, used to spot overbought and oversold conditions.

### Resistance
A technical analysis concept describing a price level where rising prices tend to stall — usually compared with previous highs.

### Return on Investment (ROI)
A metric for measuring an investment's efficiency — the ratio of net profit to net cost.

### Revenge Trading
A psychological pattern in which traders try to recover recent losses through impulsive trades.

### Risk Premium
The extra return investors demand for taking on higher levels of risk.

### Roadmap
A flexible business plan outlining a company's short- and long-term goals over an estimated timeline.

### Routing Attack
An attack at the internet service provider level that disrupts uptime or participation in web-based systems like blockchains.

### Rug pull
When a project's development team suddenly abandons it and removes or sells its liquidity, leaving holders with worthless tokens.

---

## S

### Sahm Rule
An indicator used to detect the early stages of a recession.

### Sandwich Trading
A trading or manipulation strategy in crypto markets where transactions are placed around a target trade to exploit its price impact.

### Satoshi
The smallest unit of Bitcoin — one-hundred-millionth of a BTC, or 0.00000001 BTC.

### Satoshi Nakamoto
The pseudonym used by the unknown creator (or creators) of Bitcoin.

### Secure Asset Fund for Users (SAFU)
An emergency insurance fund created by Binance in 2018 to protect users.

### Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC)
The US agency responsible for regulating securities markets.

### Security Audit
A systematic evaluation of how secure a system, smart contract, or blockchain is against attacks and technical failures.

### Security Token Offering (STO)
A fundraising method based on issuing tokens that represent securities.

### Seed Phrase
A list of words used to recover access to a cryptocurrency wallet. Sometimes called a mnemonic seed.

### Seed Tag
A label used to classify assets that are in their earliest stages of development and carry higher risk.

### Segregated Witness (SegWit)
A Bitcoin protocol upgrade aimed at improving scalability and addressing security issues.

### Selfish Mining
A strategy where a miner deliberately withholds or releases blocks to gain a competitive edge.

### Sell Wall
A large sell order — or several sell orders at the same price — sitting in an asset's order book.

### Sentiment
The collective mood of a community toward a cryptocurrency or financial market.

### Sharding
A method of splitting blockchains (or databases) into smaller, partitioned chains that handle specific data segments.

### Sharpe Ratio
A 1966 metric investors use to evaluate the potential return of an investment relative to its risk.

### Sidechains
Independent blockchains linked to a parent chain, designed to support scalability and asset transfers between networks.

### Simple Moving Average (SMA)
A technical analysis tool that smooths price data to make trends easier to identify.

### Slashing
A penalty mechanism used in proof-of-stake blockchains to discourage malicious validator behaviour.

### Slippage
When a trade is executed at a price different from what was expected — common in low-liquidity markets and with market orders.

### Smart Contract
Self-executing code stored on a blockchain network. Its terms are written directly into the code.

### Smart Contract Wallet
A wallet that uses programmable smart contracts to enable advanced functionality and stronger security.

### Snapshot
A recording of a blockchain ledger, storage device, or computer system's state at a specific moment in time.

### Social Recovery Wallet
A crypto wallet that lets users recover access using trusted contacts, in case keys or passwords are lost.

### Social Trading
A strategy that lets people mimic the trades of experienced investors and market professionals, often in real time.

### SocialFi
A blend of decentralised finance and social media, applying DeFi principles to social platforms.

### Soft Landing
When an economy slows gradually after a period of rapid growth, avoiding a recession.

### Solidity
A programming language designed specifically for writing smart contracts on Ethereum.

### Source Code
Computer code that defines how a piece of software behaves.

### SPL
Short for Solana Program Library — the rules and protocols governing how tokens behave on the Solana network.

### SRC-20 Tokens
Tokens that use Bitcoin Stamps technology to expand the utility of the Bitcoin blockchain.

### Stablecoin
A cryptocurrency designed to maintain a stable value rather than experiencing significant price swings.

### Stagflation
An economic state combining slow growth, fewer jobs, and rising prices.

### Staking Pool
A pool where stakeholders combine their staking power to improve their chances of validating new blocks.

### State Channel
A two-way communication channel between users or nodes on a network — or between a user and a service.

### stETH
A token designed to make Ethereum staking more flexible and accessible to investors.

### Stock Variable
The quantity of an economic resource measured at a single point in time.

### Store of Value
An asset that can be saved and exchanged in the future without losing value over time.

### Supercomputer
A computer (or virtual machine) operating at the highest level of computing power currently achievable.

### Supply Chain
The network of people and businesses involved in producing and delivering a product or service.

### Support
A technical analysis concept describing a price level where a falling asset tends to find buyers — usually compared with previous lows.

### Sybil Attack
A cyber attack where one entity creates many fake identities to gain disproportionate influence on a network.

## T

### Taker
A trader who places an order that fills immediately against an existing order on the book.

### Tank
A term borrowed from traditional markets, describing strong negative performance of an asset.

### Testnet
A replica of a mainnet that lets developers safely explore and test blockchain features.

### The Graph (GRT)
A decentralised protocol focused on making blockchain data more accessible.

### Ticker Symbol
A short code representing a stock, cryptocurrency, or other asset.

### Token
A digital unit issued on a blockchain. Tokens can carry value or be redeemable for assets — not to be confused with coins.

### Token Generation Event (TGE)
A fundraising method used by blockchain projects to launch tokens and raise capital.

### Token Lockup
A period in which tokens or coins cannot be transferred or traded. Also called a vesting period.

### Token Merge
The consolidation of two or more crypto tokens into a single unified token.

### Token Sale
The issuance of tokens in exchange for another cryptocurrency. Also referred to as an ICO.

### Token Standards
Sets of rules — like ERC-20, ERC-721, BEP-20, and TRC-20 — that define how tokens behave on blockchain networks.

### Tokenization
Converting real-world assets such as property, art, or financial instruments into digital tokens on a blockchain.

### Tokenomics
A combination of "token" and "economics," covering how a token works and the factors that influence its value.

### Total Supply
The total number of coins or tokens that currently exist — including those in circulation and those locked.

### Total Value Locked (TVL)
A metric measuring the total value of assets deposited in a DeFi protocol.

### TradFi
Short for "traditional finance" — the established financial system most people interact with daily.

### Transaction ID (TXID)
A unique string of characters identifying a specific transaction on a blockchain.

### Transactions Per Second (TPS)
The number of transactions a blockchain network can process each second.

### Treasury Bills (T-Bills)
Short-term financial instruments issued by governments to raise funds.

### TrueUSD (TUSD)
A US dollar-pegged stablecoin integrated with multiple blockchain networks.

### Trustless
A system where no single entity has authority, and consensus is reached without requiring participants to trust each other.

### Turing Complete
A machine that, with enough time, memory, and instructions, can solve any computational problem.

## U

### Understanding CZ's Number 4
A reference to one of CZ's New Year's resolutions: to ignore FUD, fake news, and attacks on Binance.

### Unit of Account
One of the primary functions of money — providing a standard way to measure and compare value.

### Unspent Transaction Output (UTXO)
A transaction output that must be referenced in a future transaction in order to spend the funds.

### User Interface (UI)
The space where human and machine interaction takes place, defining how users engage with a system.

### Utility Token
A token issued on a blockchain, typically through an ICO, IEO, or TGE, that grants access to a product or service.

## V

### Verification Code
A code sent to a secondary device during login, used in two-factor authentication.

### Virtual Machine
An emulated computer or distributed system that replicates the features of a physical computer's architecture.

### Vladimir Club
A nickname for someone who holds 0.01% (one-hundredth of one percent) of a cryptocurrency's maximum supply.

### Volatility
A measure of how quickly and how much an asset's price changes — calculated using standard deviations of returns.

### Volume
The number of individual units of an asset traded in a market over a given time period.

## W

### WAGMI
Crypto acronym for "We're All Gonna Make It," used to convey optimism within the community.

### Wallet
A tool for sending and receiving cryptocurrencies. Comes in several types — software, hardware, and paper.

### Wash Trading
A deceptive practice involving simultaneous buying and selling of an asset to create artificial market activity.

### Weak Hands
Traders or investors who lack the conviction to stick to their positions or trading plans.

### Weak Subjectivity
The requirement for certain nodes to depend on other nodes when determining the current state of a proof-of-stake blockchain.

### Web 1.0
The first iteration of the web, mostly read-only pages connected by hyperlinks.

### Wei
The smallest possible denomination of ether — frequently used when expressing gas prices.

### Weighted Moving Average (WMA)
A technical analysis tool that gives greater weight to recent price changes.

### Whale
A person or organisation holding a large amount of Bitcoin or another cryptocurrency — large enough to move markets.

### Whiskers
The thin lines extending from a candlestick's body, showing the full low-high range during the period. Also known as wicks or shadows.

### Whitelist
A list of approved or trusted individuals, programs, or wallet addresses for a service or event.

### Wick
A line on a candlestick chart showing where an asset's price fluctuated relative to its open and close.

### Win Rate
A metric showing how often a trader's positions turn a profit.

### Wrapped Ether (WETH)
An ERC-20 token representing Ether at a 1:1 ratio, allowing ETH to be traded with ERC-20 tokens on decentralised platforms.

### Wyckoff
A trading method created by Richard Wyckoff in the 1930s. It has had a lasting influence on modern technical analysis.

## Y

### Yield Farming
A high-risk DeFi practice where investors lock up assets to provide liquidity, lend, or stake them in exchange for rewards.

## Z

### Zero-Knowledge Proofs
Cryptographic proofs that confirm a transaction is valid without revealing any of its underlying details — preserving privacy while maintaining legitimacy.

### Zk-rollup
A layer-2 scaling solution that increases a blockchain's transaction throughput without sacrificing security.

### zk-SNARKs
Short for "Zero-Knowledge Succinct Non-Interactive Argument of Knowledge" — one approach to creating zero-knowledge proofs.

### zk-STARKs
Zero-knowledge proofs that let one party (the prover) confirm data or computations to another party (the verifier) without revealing the underlying information.
